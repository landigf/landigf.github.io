2. Lessons learned from the past

This section focuses on the foundational principles derived from traditional relational database systems.

Theory: Data independence means that the logical view on the data is cleanly separated, decoupled, from its physical storage. A RDBMS exposes a table as logical model, together with logical building block for manipulating it.
  • (HS18, FS21) Codd's 1970 paper introduced which concepts? (relational algebra, data independence).
  • (HS17, HS18) Questions on Relational Algebra equations and their validity under set and bag semantics.
  • (HS17, HS18) Questions about normal forms and their properties.
  • (HS20) What does each letter in ACID stand for?
  • (FS21) In "ACID database" terminology, what does the A stand for? (Atomicity).
  • (HS20, HS21) Is the SQL language declarative, procedural, or functional?
